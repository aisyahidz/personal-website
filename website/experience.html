My horseriding story

I started riding at 13, at AMS Equestrian Club in Serdang, a place where dust and sunlight weave into memories. My father often brought me to the stables; watching him move with the horses planted a quiet longing in me. The first time I climbed up, my hands trembled with fear and excitement. I wasn't confident but he stood beside me, patient and steady. It was that steady presence that made me try again and again.

One of my fondest days was an outride along the Pahang coastline where sand beneath the horse's hooves, the sea wind pulling at our clothes, and the horizon a long promise. The rhythm of the ride felt like a slow conversation between rider and mount.

"Trust is built slowly; confidence comes from calmness."

Lessons from Riding

  1. Trust must be built, not demanded.
  2. Confidence grows from calmness.
  3. Communication isn't always words.
